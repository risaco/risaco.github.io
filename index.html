About

I'm a pretty awesome, somewhat nerdy, super-friendly wife and mother of one. I love puzzles, sci-fi book series, the Skittles in the purple bag, and ALL things Harry Potter. I speak Math fluently, French well enough to survive, and I can read enough Spanish to get the picture. I enjoy building the backend to front-end applications, as well as making applications that are nice to look at. My goal is to build useful applications to help people in need of assistance, whether that is problem-solving, tutoring, community service, or whatever that need may be.

I fell in love with coding while working on a process improvement project at a previous job -- I discovered that developing web applications speaks to both my extremely logical rocket-scientist brain and my uber-creative aesthetic design brain at the same time. Knowing that I assisted in creating a tool that would help people gave me such a huge burst of positive energy! I earned a MERN stack certificate from the University of Kansas, and I've taken a full leap into the tech world -- best career decision to date!

I am currently a software engineer at VeriShip. What do I do? Backend software applications! Most of what my team works on is C#/.NET applications, web scrapers, SQL and noSQL databases, and hitting API endpoints. What I love about my company is that we encourage teams to research new tech and figure how to use it to make what we do better. That being said, I have also been making things in Python, Node.js, and Go! I enjoy being able to learn new things daily and make stuff that matters to people, and I'm thrilled this my life! I even hit my first devcon (KCDC) -- and it was awesome to meet great techy people and learn awesome techy things. #TechIsLife

When I’m not coding, I’m most likely adding pieces to one of my two jewelry shops (ATILTKC and AFROMOTIF), chasing my toddler, planning my next adventure, or I might even be vending at a local KC event! I am also a private tutor for high school and college math. For fun, I enjoy playing kickball, traveling, and finding great mom & pop eateries to explore (NOM!).
